On the Recursive Modelling of the Human Telencephalon

Elias Verne
Independent Researcher
Submitted: 14 May 2019
Status: Withdrawn

Abstract

This paper explores a formal approach to modelling recursive cognitive processes within the human telencephalon. Rather than focusing on semantic interpretation or phenomenological description, the proposed framework emphasizes structural recurrence, iterative constraints, and minimal representational units.

Introduction

Contemporary models of cognition frequently privilege meaning, intention, and representation. However, recent developments in computational neuroscience suggest that higher-order cognitive functions may emerge from simpler recursive structures that precede semantic attribution.

The telencephalon, as the primary locus of higher cognitive integration, provides a compelling substrate for examining such recursive patterns. This work proposes that structure itself may be the primary carrier of cognitive coherence.

Methodological Framework

The methodology adopted in this study deliberately avoids interpretive semantics. Instead, it relies on formal constraints, repetition, and invariant structural relationships. These relationships are expressed through discrete units that can be enumerated, ordered, and recombined without reference to linguistic meaning.

Discussion

Preliminary analysis indicates that recursive stability is preserved even when semantic content is removed entirely. This supports the hypothesis that structure, rather than meaning, constitutes the primary organizing principle of complex cognition.

Withdrawal Notice
This manuscript was withdrawn prior to formal peer review. The author determined that certain aspects of the work required further verification before public dissemination.
